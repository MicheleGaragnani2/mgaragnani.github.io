Michele Garagnani

Associate Professor (Senior Lecturer) · University of Melbourne  |  Centre for Brain, Mind & Markets

I am a neuroeconomist and behavioural economist interested in how people make decisions, why they make mistakes, and how to measure their true preferences. My work combines experimental methods, EEG/fMRI, and response-time analysis to study the cognitive and neural underpinnings of choice.

Download CV Google Scholar ORCID

Research Interests

Neuroeconomics Behavioral Economics Judgment & Decision Making Stochastic Choice Response Times EEG / fMRI Strength of Preference Bayesian Updating Pain Measurement

A central thread of my research is developing and validating methods to reveal people's preferences and predict their choices using non-choice data, particularly using response times and neural signals. This has led to contributions across decision theory, experimental economics, and neuroeconomics, with applications ranging from risky choice to cooperation, belief updating, voting, and the measurement of subjective feelings.
